About 15-Minute Cities

The concept of a 15-minute city is an urban planning model that aims to create cities where all essential services and amenities are within a 15-minute walk or bike ride from residents' homes. This approach promotes sustainability, reduces reliance on cars, and enhances the quality of life for urban dwellers.

Key Features

  • Accessibility: All essential services such as schools, healthcare, parks, and grocery stores are easily accessible.
  • Sustainability: Encourages walking and cycling, reducing carbon emissions.
  • Community Engagement: Fosters a sense of community by promoting local businesses and social interactions.

Benefits

  • Improved health and well-being through increased physical activity.
  • Reduced traffic congestion and pollution.
  • Enhanced local economies by supporting small businesses.

Conclusion

The 15-minute city model is a forward-thinking approach to urban planning that prioritizes the needs of residents while promoting sustainability and community engagement.
